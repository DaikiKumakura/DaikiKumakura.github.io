RESEARCH (My project)

Mutually Beneficial Specialization and Bacterial Proliferation Model

Picture this: bacteria engaged in a whimsical dance of asexual reproduction. One becomes two, two become four, and the saga of multiplication unfolds. We dub this spectacle "proliferation," a lively performance that comes at the cost of energy consumption. These energetic endeavors of bacteria encompass a medley of microbial tasks - think flagella-fueled frolics and fluorescent feats.


But pause for thought: does our conventional understanding of bacterial growth align with their vivacious work ethic? In this captivating study, we venture into the microscopic world, assuming that bacteria engage in not one, but two distinct "jobs" within their bustling community. Armed with a straightforward model, we embark on a journey to ponder the whimsical wonders of specialization at the bacterial collective level.

Plasmid Propagation and Establishment of Antibiotic Resistance

Once upon a time, bacterial infections could be cured with the magic of antibiotics. The past tense arises due to the recent emergence of antibiotic-resistant microbes. These resistant strains spring forth from various causes, with plasmid-borne resistance being particularly captivating. Within these plasmids, antibiotic resistance genes are tucked away like notes in a musical cassette, ready to be copy-pasted for a symphony of survival. The alarming twist is that this exchange isn't confined to the same bacterial species. It's a cross-species affair, taking place not just within organisms but also on the grand stage of the environment, such as rivers. This environmental theater offers an easy breeding ground for these genetic escapades, making swift countermeasures a pressing need.

　　
But amidst this molecular drama, an urgency remains obscured. The transmission of plasmids and the emergence of resistant strains have found a limited spotlight, mostly restricted to the laboratory's glass confines and mathematical equations. This oversight in plasmid dissemination and resistance's establishment means the prediction of resistant microbes becomes a tangled riddle. Left unresolved, this puzzle could foretell a challenging future. In this scientific odyssey, we embark on crafting a straightforward model to unveil these complexities. Armed with the spirit of pioneers, we unravel the conditions under which plasmid-acquired resistance thrives and the potent brew of antibiotic exposure required to quell these resilient rebels. The narrative unfolds with a harmonious aim – to choreograph a solution that restores the equilibrium of life, casting aside the looming shadows of resistance.
